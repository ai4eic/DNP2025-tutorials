References and Resources#

Research Papers#

Deep Learning in High Energy Physics#

General ML in HEP:

  • Guest, D., Cranmer, K., & Whiteson, D. (2018). “Deep Learning and its Application to LHC Physics.” Annual Review of Nuclear and Particle Science, 68, 161-181. arXiv:1806.11484

  • Albertsson, K., et al. (2018). “Machine Learning in High Energy Physics Community White Paper.” Journal of Physics: Conference Series, 1085(2), 022008. arXiv:1807.02876

  • Bourilkov, D. (2019). “Machine and Deep Learning Applications in Particle Physics.” International Journal of Modern Physics A, 34(35), 1930019. arXiv:1912.08245

Classification and Tagging:

  • Baldi, P., Sadowski, P., & Whiteson, D. (2014). “Searching for Exotic Particles in High-Energy Physics with Deep Learning.” Nature Communications, 5, 4308. arXiv:1402.4735

  • Kasieczka, G., et al. (2019). “The Machine Learning Landscape of Top Taggers.” SciPost Physics, 7, 014. arXiv:1902.09914

  • Louppe, G., Kagan, M., & Cranmer, K. (2017). “Learning to Pivot with Adversarial Networks.” Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 30. arXiv:1611.01046

Generative Models in Physics#

GANs for Calorimeter Simulation:

  • Paganini, M., de Oliveira, L., & Nachman, B. (2018). “CaloGAN: Simulating 3D High Energy Particle Showers in Multi-Layer Electromagnetic Calorimeters with Generative Adversarial Networks.” Physical Review D, 97(1), 014021. arXiv:1705.02355

  • Paganini, M., de Oliveira, L., & Nachman, B. (2018). “Accelerating Science with Generative Adversarial Networks: An Application to 3D Particle Showers in Multi-Layer Calorimeters.” Physical Review Letters, 120(4), 042003. arXiv:1701.05927

  • Erdmann, M., et al. (2019). “Generating and Refining Particle Detector Simulations Using the Wasserstein Distance in Adversarial Networks.” Computing and Software for Big Science, 2, 4. arXiv:1802.03325

VAEs and Flow Models:

  • Deja, K., et al. (2021). “End-to-end Sinkhorn Autoencoder with Noise Generator.” IEEE Access, 9, 36436-36450. arXiv:2006.06704

  • Kansal, R., et al. (2021). “Particle Cloud Generation with Message Passing Generative Adversarial Networks.” NeurIPS Workshop on Machine Learning and the Physical Sciences. arXiv:2106.11535

Diffusion Models:

  • Mikuni, V., & Nachman, B. (2021). “Score-based Generative Models for Calorimeter Shower Simulation.” Physical Review D, 106(9), 092009. arXiv:2206.11898

Physics-Informed ML#

  • Cranmer, K., et al. (2020). “The Frontier of Simulation-Based Inference.” Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 117(48), 30055-30062. arXiv:1911.01429

  • Brehmer, J., Cranmer, K., Louppe, G., & Pavez, J. (2019). “A Guide to Constraining Effective Field Theories with Machine Learning.” Physical Review D, 98(5), 052004. arXiv:1805.00020

GlueX Experiment#

GlueX Physics:

  • GlueX Collaboration. (2015). “The GlueX Detector at Jefferson Lab.” Nuclear Instruments and Methods in Physics Research Section A, 799, 1-24.

  • Adhikari, S., et al. (2021). “The GlueX Beamline and Detector.” Nuclear Instruments and Methods in Physics Research Section A, 987, 164807. arXiv:2005.14272

Exotic Mesons:

  • Meyer, C. A., & Swanson, E. S. (2015). “Hybrid Mesons.” Progress in Particle and Nuclear Physics, 82, 21-58. arXiv:1502.07276

Electromagnetic Calorimetry#

Fundamentals:

  • Fabjan, C. W., & Gianotti, F. (2003). “Calorimetry for Particle Physics.” Reviews of Modern Physics, 75(4), 1243-1286.

  • Wigmans, R. (2008). Calorimetry: Energy Measurement in Particle Physics (2nd ed.). Oxford University Press.

  • Particle Data Group. (2022). “Passage of Particles Through Matter.” In Review of Particle Physics. pdg.lbl.gov
